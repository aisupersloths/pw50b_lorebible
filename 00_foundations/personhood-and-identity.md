# Personhood and Identity

## Summary

The setting treats personhood as real but plastic. Memory-sharing complicates identity without abolishing it.

## Canon

- A person may exist as a singleton, a member of a mind-tribe, a member of a mind-club, an upload, or something adjacent to those forms.
- Mind-tribes are typically near-identical wetware sharing memories.
- Mind-clubs are typically non-identical individuals who choose synchronized memory-sharing.
- Re-syncing does not always collapse divergence; multiple versions may be retained.
- There is no hard boundary between biological and synthetic intelligence.
- There is no hard boundary between ordinary intelligence and superintelligence.
- Clones are usually instantiated as adults.
- Children created for ordinary reproduction are distinct persons with genuine childhoods.

## Human shorthand

In this corpus, `human` is functional shorthand for beings broadly comparable to present humans in mental and usually physical constraints.
It is not a claim that they are recognizably Homo sapiens.

## Descended diversity

- These beings may descend from humans, dogs, chimps, elephants, dolphins, octopi, and other Earth-origin lineages.
- They may be biologically unrecognizable to us.
- They do not generally know that they share deep common ancestry.
