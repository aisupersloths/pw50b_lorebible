# Themes

## Summary

The setting is ambiguous rather than utopian or dystopian.

## Canon

- Tone should not collapse into pure utopia or pure dystopia.
- Stories should favor ordinary life with larger philosophical, political, and economic themes interwoven.
- The setting should feel ancient, layered, and archaeologically deep.
- Relatable viewpoints matter even when the surrounding institutions and histories are alien.
- Quiet horror, procedural strangeness, and buried moral complexity fit the setting.

## Strong recurring themes

- continuity vs drift
- plural personhood
- memory as infrastructure
- history as capital
- archaeology as innovation
- local worlds under distant superintelligent pressure
- embodied lives retaining value in post-scarcity conditions
