# Cosmology

## Canon

- Wormholes exist and are stable enough to matter.
- Wormholes are costly to use.
- Wormholes are generally controlled by inscrutable superhuman intelligences.
- Wormholes weakly bind galactic culture behind the scenes.
- Wormholes are generally not practical everyday transit for viewpoint characters.
- Distance from wormhole-connected worlds loosely correlates with technological divergence.

## Implications

- Local societies can remain highly divergent despite belonging to a larger galactic ecosystem.
- A connected galactic culture exists, but most ordinary people experience it indirectly.
- Worlds can specialize economically and epistemically without becoming culturally uniform.
