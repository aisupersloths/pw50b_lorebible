# Glossary

## singleton
A person who is not currently part of a shared memory-set with other active persons.

## mind-tribe
A memory-sharing social unit composed primarily of near-identical wetware, often clone-based.

## mind-club
A memory-sharing social unit composed of non-identical individuals.

## memory-set
A currently shared body of synchronized or semi-synchronized memory counted as one shared identity archive for the purposes of social analysis.

## upload
An originally biological mind running in a non-biological substrate. Uploads can usually share memory-sets with embodied people, and can therefore be part of the same mind-club or mind-tribe.

## ascension
A transition out of ordinary embodied existence into a more advanced form that usually becomes inaccessible or incomprehensible to ordinary minds.

## ghost
An agentic personality fragment or residual structure carried within large memory corpora; technological, not supernatural. They can be thought of as unintentional specs.

## spec
A mind derived from archived, copied, merged, edited, or contract-grown cognitive substrate and optimized for a specific task, environment, or role. Short for specialist. Specs can vary in sentience from completely non-sentient to superhuman in their intelligence. Since the same technology is used by people to self-modify, there is no clear boundary between people and specs but the more narrowly focused and obedient a being is, the more likely it is to be treated as a spec.

## spec engineering
The deliberate design or modification of volition, drives, preferences, behavioral tendencies, motives, loyalties, inhibitions, obedience, curiosity, patience, risk tolerance, or similar aspects of a spec (or in principle a person, since the methods are the same).

## cognitive template
A reusable mind-derived pattern, memory-set, or structured cognitive starting point used to produce new specialist sentiences.

## bod
Short for embodiment platform. A biological and/or mechanical body designed to host a downloaded or otherwise deployed mind, usually temporarily and usually can be swapped between different minds. A biological body modified to be used by multiple minds instead of a single primary one might also be considered a bod. On the other hand a prosthetic body used by only one mind would just be called a body, or maybe a permanent bod.

## reintegration
The merger of multiple minds. They can be embodied or non-embodied at the time of the merger. The more similar they were when they were last forked, the easier the process is. This is the process underlying mind-tribes, mind-clubs, as well as the creation and auditing of specs. When the intent is transferring knowledge or experiences, it's technically a type of reintegration but is usually called memory-sharing or meming.
