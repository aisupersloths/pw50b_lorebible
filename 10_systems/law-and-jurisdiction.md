# Law and Jurisdiction

## Canon

- Law varies by culture and jurisdiction.
- Near-identical clone families and larger mind-tribes are often treated as single entities for property and political participation.
- Heterogeneous mind-clubs tend to receive more recognition of individual property and political influence.
- There is no single galaxy-wide legal regime.
- Jurisdictions vary sharply on the status of specialist sentiences and edited branches.
- Jurisdictions vary on who may authorize copying, volitional editing, deployment, reintegration, retirement, or deletion.
- Consent rules for edited or economically deployed minds are not uniform.
- Liability for the acts of deployed copies or specialist sentiences is a major axis of legal variation.

## Implications

- Personhood is not uniform.
- Ownership, inheritance, backup legality, and representation vary locally.
- Legal structure is a major axis of world differentiation.
- Some jurisdictions treat specialist sentiences as persons.
- Some treat them as property, contracts, or intermediate statuses.
- Cross-jurisdiction disputes over identity, consent, and liability are common design space for the setting.
