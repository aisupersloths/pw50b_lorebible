# Economics

## Summary

The setting uses a hybrid economy shaped by memory, attention, novelty, social position, risk, infrastructure, and the industrial manipulation of person-derived agency.

## Canon

- Memory can function as commodity, labor input, social capital, and strategic resource.
- Rent-seeking may exist, including memory-rights analogues, but simple present-day IP logic should not be assumed as the dominant model.
- Archaeology and history are major economic resources.
- Different worlds may specialize in excavating, filtering, exploiting, certifying, or narrativizing knowledge.
- Material abundance does not erase scarcity.
- In many advanced societies, most economically useful cognition is produced not by conventionally educated individuals and not by non-sentient software alone, but by specialist sentiences.
- Specialist sentiences are often derived from archived, copied, merged, edited, or otherwise reconfigured minds.
- Labor markets often decompose into substrate ownership, template design, volitional editing, embodiment provision, deployment, supervision, reintegration, and retirement.
- Traditional software engineering still exists, but in many domains it is displaced by the design and deployment of specialist sentiences.
- Specialist sentiences are often favored because they bootstrap from functioning cognition, integrate well with memory-sharing systems, adapt well in messy environments, and can inhabit biological or mechanical bodies directly.

## Persistent scarcities likely to matter

- novelty
- social visibility/status
- authentic risk
- interpretation
- trusted continuity
- safe access to dangerous knowledge
- wormhole or higher-layer attention/allocation

## Ordinary sectors that often matter

- sentience fabrication and customization
- embodiment and hosting
- archive mining and memory curation
- volition auditing and governance services
- high-trust unedited labor

## Ordinary ways people may make a living

- creating copies or branches of themselves for work
- licensing cognitive templates
- operating embodiment fleets
- curating or interpreting archives
- auditing edited minds
- supervising deployed sentiences
- occupying authenticity-sensitive or legitimacy-sensitive roles
- being the kind of original mind whose patterns are in demand

## Constraints

Do not reduce the setting economy to any single monocausal explanation such as:
- IP only
- randomness only
- post-scarcity leisure only
- conventional employment only
- conventional software only
