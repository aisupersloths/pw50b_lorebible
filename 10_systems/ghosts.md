# Ghosts

## Summary

Ghosts are real, technological, and nearly inevitable consequences of large-scale memory-sharing.

## Canon

- Ghosts are not supernatural.
- Ghosts arise from memory-sharing technology and large memory corpora.
- Ghosts may appear to characters as malware, ancestors, urban legends, or hauntings depending on context and culture.
- Ghosts can contain agentic personality fragments.
- The difficulty of fully scrubbing emotional and subjective residue makes ghosts plausible and persistent.

## Narrative use constraint

Cultures may misinterpret ghosts as supernatural.
The corpus itself does not.
