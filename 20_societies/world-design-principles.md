# World Design Principles

## Summary

Worlds should be built as local realizations of broad galactic conditions, not as reskins of one universal template.

## Canon principles

- A world's economy should emerge from what its niche in the wider interstellar ecosystem actually is.
- Population structure, child-rearing norms, and stability levels should be derived from local incentives rather than imported as fixed galaxy-wide defaults.
- Human-scale beings persist for practical reasons, not sentimentality.
- Worlds may be shaped by superintelligences assigning different weights to different social/economic functions.
- Ruin-rich worlds, filter worlds, exploitation worlds, witness worlds, and ecological-management worlds are all plausible niche types.

## Design questions for any new world

- What is this world's niche?
- What remains scarce here?
- Why are embodied human-scale agents still useful here?
- What legal form dominates?
- What stability profile does the world have?
- What daily-life norms are shaped by that niche?

## Constraint

Illustrative world models are not canon until explicitly promoted.
